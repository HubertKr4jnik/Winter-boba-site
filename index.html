Christmas Log

If you are not a fan of Christmas trees you might find this Spanish tradition intresting. In many parts of this country gifts are brought by Tió de Nadal which means Christmas Log. It's a peace of wood often decorated with little legs and a face. The Log also gets fed and covered in it's own blanket at night. Here's where it get's unique - On Christmas Eve Tió gets put in the fireplace and beaten with sticks untill it... poops presents and candies🍬

Christmas Pickle

In some countries a Christmas pickle is your favourite festive decoration. For example in Germany a pickle-shaped ornament is put on the Christmas tree. The first person to find this unique ornament gets and extra present or good fortune. The exact origin of this isn't known but it's alright when it gets you an opportunity to earn an extra gift 😜

Christmas Spiders

Don't freak out - it's just a decoration 🕷️. In the Ukraine Christmas trees are decorated with ornaments in the shape of spiders or spider webs. It's said that they bring good luck. It's believed that this tradition goes way back to when a poor woman couldn't afford any ornaments to put onto her Christmas tree. When she woke up the next mornign the tree was covered in spider webs that sparkled in the sun

Christmas Chicken

Christmas is all about food. Believe it or not but the traditional Japanese meal is... KFC. That's right Kentucky Fried Chicken. Each year people flock to the American fast food chain to get "Kurisumasu ni wa kentakkii!" - “Kentucky For Christmas!”. This is the result of a marketing campaign in 1974 🍗
